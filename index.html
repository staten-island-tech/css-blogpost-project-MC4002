Flowers in Chinese Culture

Plum Blossoms

Also known by its scientific name, prunus mume, the plum blossoms are considered to be China's national flower. These flowers are said to represent endurance and growing through hardships due to the fact that these plum blossoms generally blossom in winter.

Peony

The peony or paeonia suffruticosa is considered to be China's most significant flower and has played a major role in numerous holidays and traditions. This flower symbolizes authority, prosperity, wealth and honor.

Chinese Rose

The Chinese Rose is a beautiful color-varying blossom with a gentle, sweet fragance and blooms all year. This flower is thought to imply a powerful spirit and a strong will that will lead to success.
